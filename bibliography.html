Preface:

Marijuana has become one of the most highly debated topics of the 21st century, especially in the United States where legislation is being passed in its favor. Part of the importance of this subject lies in the profound ripple effect that marijuana has on our environment. This part of the marijuana industry is not commonly discussed, yet it is critical for the future of the cannabis business and well as the future of our Earth. Because of this, I have chosen to study the environmental effects of the legalization of marijuana to promote awareness on this part of the topic that is not talked about very often.

As more regions around the world move towards marijuana legalization, understanding the environmental consequences becomes pivotal. It is not merely a matter of legality but a profound environmental concern that touches upon issues like land use, water consumption, energy consumption, waste management, and more. The urgency of addressing these challenges is further magnified by the escalating global environmental crisis.

The genre of this project will be a website. I am a Computer Science major; therefore, I have lots of experience with coding and web design. I would love to apply these skills to environmental biology. I plan to publish my website using a public domain so that everyone can access my project. My audience is not anyone in particular – just people who have an interest in the topic of marijuana and would like to learn more about its effects on the environment. I would say that my audience would be readers in the United States, however, because I plan on discussing lots of American legislature. This also means that my audience could also include lawmakers who may need to learn more about the issue.

As previously stated, the primary purpose of this project is to inform. The goal is to provide a comprehensive understanding of the environmental consequences associated with the legalization of marijuana. By presenting a well-researched and balanced perspective, I aim to empower readers with the knowledge necessary to engage in informed discussions, advocate for sustainable practices, and influence policymaking in this domain.

My observations and conclusions will be drawn from accredited sources (included below), giving me the authority to write about this topic. I have kept up to date with legislation surrounding this topic for years, which will add to my knowledge of the topic along with these sources. Additionally, I have studied environmental changes for many years, both in my high school science classes as well as my environmental biology class at Providence College.
