Pollution

Growing cannabis can leave behind traces of light, air, and noise pollution, impacting both the environment and the communities that surround these cultivation sites.
  • Light pollution:
    • The light pollution that comes from marijuana farms can cause behavioral and psychological changes in local wildlife, including:
      • Sleep cycles
      • Feeding cycles
      • Navigation
  • Air pollution:
    • Biogenic volatile organic compounds have been linked to marijuana cultivation
      • Which can cause harm to the ozone layer
      • However, this is the case for many other crops
    • Research is somewhat lacking regarding the effects of marijuana farming on the environment compared to other crops
  • Noise pollution:
    • Innovative farming equipment and technology, while useful, can cause noise that influences local wildlife, including:
      • Interference with sleep
      • Effect on communication or speech interference
      • Effect on hearing/deafness
