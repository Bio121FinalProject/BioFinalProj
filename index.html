Marijuana and the Environment

Marijuana has become one of the most highly debated topics of the 21st century, especially in the United States where legislation is being passed in its favor. Part of the importance of this subject lies in the profound ripple effect that marijuana has on our environment. This part of the marijuana industry is not commonly discussed, yet it is critical for the future of the cannabis business and well as the future of our Earth.

Water

Cannabis, being an extremely water-intensive crop, has the potential to deplete local bodies of water, thereby posing a threat to local wildlife and humans.

Habitat Loss

The expansion of cannabis farming areas can disrupt natural habitats, posing threats to local ecosystems and the diverse plant and animal species within them.

Pollution

Growing cannabis can leave behind traces of light, air, and noise pollution, impacting both the environment and the communities that surround these cultivation sites.

Pesticide Use

Similar to the cultivation of numerous other crops, the use of pesticides can have detrimental effects on local wildlife. Regulations on pesticides are in place in many areas, but problems still arise.
